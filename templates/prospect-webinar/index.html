One session, 20+ assets

Your team already produced all of this. You just haven't extracted it yet.

A 60-minute webinar contains enough raw material to feed your content pipeline for weeks. Most teams publish the recording and move on.

Short-form video clips

3–5 clipped moments per session. The sharp insight, the provocative question, the quotable stat. Native content for LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, social. Your speakers become visible.

Long-form written content

The transcript becomes a blog post or article with minimal editing. The key moments become a PDF summary or SlideShare. The Q&A becomes an FAQ resource. One session, multiple indexed pages.

Email nurture sequences

Key moments from the session become a 3–5 email sequence for attendees who didn't convert, and for replay viewers. Behaviour-based: what they engaged with determines what they receive next.

On-demand as a lead channel

Replay viewers convert. They just need the right follow-up.

The research is clear: replay-sourced leads convert to opportunity at nearly the same rate as live attendees (Contrast, 2026). The intent signal — choosing to watch your content — doesn't disappear because they missed the live session.

The gap isn't in the audience. It's in the follow-up infrastructure. Most teams send the replay link once and never segment on what happened next.

What good looks like: replay sent within 24 hours → segment by watch depth → different sequence for >50% vs <50% viewed → CRM sync on engagement score, not just "watched: yes".
